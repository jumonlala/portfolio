About Me

Welcome to my portfolio website!

I’m Jimin, a Junior majoring in Data Science with a minor in Music.

My passion lies at the intersection of data science, human-computer interaction, education, and mental health, where I’m driven to use technology to address societal challenges.
I began my academic journey as a pre-med student, driven by a desire to study computational neuroscience. This path led me to research at an MRI imaging lab, where I manually labeled different areas of the brain, and to a microbiology lab focused on HIV. Last summer, I undertook an independent project, developing a deep learning algorithm to automate the quality-check process of induced pluripotent stem cells.

While I thrived in the rigorous environment of scientific research, my interests gradually shifted from biological sciences to addressing societal problems, particularly those related to mental health and education.

Coming from a family of first-generation college graduates, I carry the weight of unresolved generational trauma stemming from the Korean War, famine, economic instability, and gender inequality. I arrived in the U.S. alone, unaware of the challenges ahead in self-care, identity exploration, and building a support network. However, my two years at WashU have been transformative, shaping my character, values, and mission. Engaging in civic dialogues, support groups, and diverse communities has helped me solidify my purpose: to use my knowledge and skills to improve educational experiences for disadvantaged groups and to promote awareness of mental health challenges.

My interests are interdisciplinary, much like my major. I view them as overlapping circles in a Venn diagram, with the intersections representing the public issues I am passionate about solving, one step at a time. I envision combining the technologies I’ve studied with insights from psychology, education, public health, and music to address these issues.

For example, I aspire to integrate human-computer interaction with psychology to create tools that make learning more efficient for those who struggle with motivation or information retention. I want to create interactive web visualizations that shed light on the realities of youth mental health struggles, literacy crisis, child neglect, and other public health issues. I want to develop computer interfaces that help trauma patients process their memories as events of the past, enabling them to be more present in their daily lives. I hope to create a reliable large language model that helps parents reflect on their conversations with their children, fostering honest and intimate relationships. I aim to make math more understandable through visualizations and more relevant by placing learners in the roles of professionals who solve real-world problems using mathematical knowledge. Beyond these, as someone often comforted by classical music, I want to develop online learning tools that offer interactive and intuitive ways to understand classical music.

There is much I hope to achieve, and I am excited to embark on this journey. The struggles of those facing challenges in mental health and education resonate deeply with me, and I am grateful to be in a position where I can make a meaningful difference in their lives.
